GAME 02

Ultimatum Game

One player proposes how to split a sum of money with another. The second player can accept or reject. If rejected, both get nothing. Tests fairness and rationality.

  • Test theories of fairness and equity
  • Observe cross-cultural differences
  • Analyze rejection thresholds
  • Study behavioral economics principles

GAME 03

Trust Game

Player 1 decides how much money to send to Player 2. The amount is multiplied, and Player 2 decides how much to send back. A fundamental study of trust and reciprocity.

  • Measure trust and trustworthiness
  • Study reciprocity dynamics
  • Explore social preferences
  • Analyze repeated interactions

GAME 04

Public Goods Game

Each player decides how much to contribute to a common pool. The total is multiplied and redistributed equally. Studies cooperation in groups and the free-rider problem.

  • Multi-player strategic interactions
  • Study collective action problems
  • Analyze contribution patterns
  • Test punishment mechanisms

GAME 05

Stag Hunt

Players can hunt a stag together for a large reward, or hunt a hare alone for a smaller but guaranteed reward. Explores coordination and risk in social cooperation.

  • Multiple Nash equilibria
  • Coordination problem analysis
  • Risk-dominant vs payoff-dominant strategies
  • Social contract theory applications

GAME 06

Battle of the Sexes

Two players prefer to coordinate on an activity, but each prefers a different activity. Classic coordination game with conflicting interests and multiple equilibria.

  • Coordination with preference conflicts
  • Study bargaining and compromise
  • Multiple equilibria selection

GAME 07

Chicken Game

Two players head toward each other. Each must choose to swerve or continue straight. Worst outcome is mutual destruction. Models brinkmanship and escalation dynamics.

  • Anti-coordination game dynamics
  • Study commitment and credibility
  • Escalation and deterrence theory
  • Mixed strategy equilibria

GAME 08

Hawk-Dove Game

Animals compete for a resource. Hawks fight, doves share. If two hawks fight, both get injured. Evolutionary game theory and the evolution of animal conflict.

  • Evolutionary stable strategies
  • Population dynamics simulation
  • Cost-benefit analysis in conflict
  • Biological applications of game theory
